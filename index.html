About Me

I am a bioengineering student at UC San Diego with experience in both academia and industry, focused on designing and building electromechanical systems that integrate mechanical design, embedded systems, and software. My work includes haptic devices, teleoperation systems, and medical device development, where I build complete systems from concept through testing and validation.

My interest in bioengineering began with a general desire to improve healthcare using engineering. Through coursework and hands-on projects, I became increasingly drawn to mechanical engineering, robotics, and human-robot interaction. While I continue to value healthcare applications, I am now focused on designing and building integrated systems where hardware, control, and sensing come together in real-world environments to improve how humans and technology interact with the physical world.

Selected Projects

N3D Haptic Glove

Developing the world's first wearable haptic glove capable of rendering axial, transverse, and shear forces across multiple fingertips. Designed the glove’s unique capstan-driven mechanical structure through rapid prototyping, characterized motor voltage–force relationship through mathematical modeling, and implemented ROS2-based scripts with forward kinematics for fingertip pose tracking. Enables users to experience realistic force feedback during contact-rich virtual reality and robot teleoperation tasks.

CAD · Rapid Prototyping · ROS2 · Python · Embedded Systems

View Project

Pan-Tilt Object Tracking Device

Built an autonomous real-time 360° pan-tilt object tracking device using an ESP32-CAM, continuous servo motor actuation, and a computer vision pipeline. Developed an OpenCV pipeline using HSV color segmentation and implemented PID control for closed-loop servo positioning, integrated with a custom 3D-printed mechanical design. System architecture intentionally designed to support extension toward more advanced OpenCV detection methods beyond simple color-based tracking.

Python · OpenCV · PID Control · Embedded Systems · CAD

View Project

VR Humanoid Hand Teleoperation System

Developing a ROS2-based teleoperation framework for virtual and physical humanoid hand control with integrated haptic feedback. Integrated Meta Quest hand tracking with a MuJoCo simulation to compute real-time finger-object contact forces, and mapped simulated forces to servo motor commands of a wearable haptic glove. Enables realistic, closed-loop haptic interaction during virtual object grasping tasks using entirely open-source hardware.

ROS2 · MuJoCo Simulation · Python · Embedded Systems
